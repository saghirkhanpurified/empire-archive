Universities are still training students to be "Individual Contributors"—people who sit quietly, memorize data, and do the work themselves. 

**This model is obsolete.**

According to 2026 workforce analytics, nearly 70% of professionals in cutting-edge sectors report their roles have shifted from "doing the work" to managing AI agents that do the work. The market no longer pays for execution; it pays for orchestration. 

### The "Full-Stack Manager" Protocol
If you want to survive the post-AI job market, you must stop acting like a traditional student and start acting like a **Full-Stack Manager**. 
*   **Stop memorizing:** Delegate raw data summarization to AI. 
*   **Start orchestrating:** Focus your cognitive energy on prompting, debugging, and directing AI models to solve complex, multi-step problems. 

Mid-level roles requiring AI proficiency currently command a massive salary premium. The students who win won't be the ones with the best handwritten notes; they will be the ones who manage the smartest digital agents.
